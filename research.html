Research

In mathematics, I am primarily interested in random graphs/hypergraphs and their applications. I also enjoy learning about math education, computer science (and particularly its overlap with mathematics), and discrete math in general.


Random Hypergraphs

I am currently thinking about the threshold for the appearance of perfect matchings (and, with any luck, Hamilton cycles) in k-uniform, k-partite random hypergraphs. This work is a variation on Shamir's problem, which asks, roughly, how many edges we need to include in a random hypergraph to be (asymptotically almost) sure that it contains a perfect matching.

In a collaboration started at GRWC, I worked with Calum MacRury, Tomáš Masařík, and Xavier Pérez-Giménez on studying discrepancy in two models of random hypergraph. The discrepancy of a hypergraph is, in some sense, a measure of how well we can balance a vertex-coloring over the hypergraph's edges.

Publications

STEM Education

In education research, I have worked on two projects related to elementary STEM teaching: one focused on developing STEM teacher leaders, and another on teaching computer science in particular. I have experience with interviewing K-8 teachers, conducting literature searches, and coding and further analyzing qualitative data.

My experiences as a discrete mathematician and a college math instructor have stoked my interest in studying issues related to undergraduate and graduate mathematics education, as well as teaching discrete mathematics in particular. I am especially interested in exploring how students and instructors interact in undergraduate discrete mathematics courses. This could serve as a stepping-stone to understanding the role that discrete math could play in the equitable teaching of mathematics.

Publications
